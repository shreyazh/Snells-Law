Question 1:

What happens to the direction of light when it passes from a medium with a higher refractive index to a medium with a lower refractive index?

Answer: When light passes from a medium with a higher refractive index to a medium with a lower refractive index, it undergoes refraction. Refraction is the bending of light as it passes through different mediums. The direction of light changes because it travels at different speeds in different mediums. As the speed of light changes, it bends towards the normal, which is a line perpendicular to the surface of separation between the two mediums.

Question 2:

How does the speed of light change when it enters a medium with a higher refractive index? What about in the opposite direction?

Answer: When light enters a medium with a higher refractive index, such as from air to water, its speed decreases. This decrease in speed is because the refractive index is a measure of how much slower light travels in a medium compared to a vacuum. In the opposite direction, when light goes from a medium with a higher refractive index to a lower refractive index, such as from water to air, its speed increases.

Question 4:

How does the wavelength of light change when it enters a medium with a higher refractive index?

Answer: When light enters a medium with a higher refractive index, such as from air to glass, its wavelength decreases. This phenomenon is known as wavelength reduction or wavelength shortening. The reduction in wavelength is accompanied by a decrease in the speed of light. The relationship between the wavelength and refractive index is inversely proportional: as the refractive index increases, the wavelength of light decreases.
